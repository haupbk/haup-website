ABOUT

In Cambodia, eating is a big deal. You don't just ask someone how they're doing, you ask them what they've eaten today. There are five distinct words for "eat" in the Khmer language, "haup" (pronounced hope) being the most formal, used for people you don't yet know.

Haup, a pop-up restaurant by Chef Bun Cheam and Cait Callahan, is our take on Cambodian street food with a lot of soul.

Bun, who immigrated from Cambodia with his family in 1989, retained a strong connection to the food and culture. While crafting the menu for Haup, he drew inspiration from the smells, sounds, and tastes of the street markets there, as well as his favorite street food in New York City, where he and Cait now live.

Lemongrass-and-pork stuffed chicken wings, marinated beef-on-a-stick, meatball skewers, pickled veggies, and endlessly addictive cheeseburger dumplings blend our favorite flavors together for a menu that is fun, playful, unpretentious, and damn good.

We’re popping up at bars and restaurants all around the city cooking, eating, and haup-fully making lots friends along the way. Check back here, or follow us on Instagram, Facebook, or Twitter to find out where we’ll be next.
